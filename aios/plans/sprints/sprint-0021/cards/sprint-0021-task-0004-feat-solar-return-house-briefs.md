# Solar Return — "This Year's Themes" Angular House Briefs

**Type:** Feature
**Originated by:** Jobs, Miyazaki

## Problem / Opportunity

The Solar Return reading page (`/projects/astrology-reader/src/components/results/SolarReturnPage.tsx`) already places every SR planet in a natal-style house, but the reading surface only surfaces two of them. The `SRStaticBriefs` component (lines 352–388) reads `PLANET_IN_HOUSE` for the SR Sun and SR Moon and presents them as two side-by-side cards labelled "Primary Focus" and "Emotional Climate." Every other planet — Mercury through Pluto, plus the asteroids in `PLANET_IN_HOUSE` (Chiron, Ceres, Pallas, Juno, Vesta) — has a house placement in `srChart.planets` and a corresponding `brief` in `PLANET_IN_HOUSE`, but nothing surfaces them to the user.

The consequence is a reading that is technically complete but experientially thin. A user who has Venus in SR house 7 or Saturn in SR house 10 receives no static interpretive layer for those placements — only the GPT prose, which is necessarily summary-level and may not call out every meaningful house cluster. The `PLANET_IN_HOUSE` data exists across all 120 planet-house combinations (10 planets × 12 houses, plus extended asteroid entries); the gap is purely in the reading surface, not in the data layer.

The opportunity is high leverage for low implementation cost: a curated selection of SR placements in angular houses (1, 4, 7, 10) plus the two luminaries already shown would make the Solar Return reading meaningfully more useful as a self-study tool, fitting the sprint-0021 mandate to deepen the static reading layer.

## Vision

When a user opens the Solar Return Reading tab, they see — beneath the existing two-card `SRStaticBriefs` row and above the GPT prose — a section titled **"This Year's Themes."** It is a curated set of brief cards, not a full-planet dump. Each card names a planet, its house, and the one-line `brief` from `PLANET_IN_HOUSE`, framed as a year-theme rather than a natal character description. The cards feel like a focused shortlist: typically two to five items depending on the user's SR chart. The section does not exist at all if the chart produces no qualifying placements beyond what `SRStaticBriefs` already shows.

The signature moment: a user whose SR has Mars in house 10 and Jupiter in house 1 immediately sees two distinct year-theme cards — "Ambition drives this year" and "Expansive new chapter begins" — without having to decode the planet table themselves. The reading feels curated by an astrologer who looked at the chart and circled what matters, not generated by an algorithm that listed everything.

The overall page order in the Reading tab becomes:
1. `SRStaticBriefs` — Sun and Moon (always first; already shipped)
2. **"This Year's Themes"** — angular placements + any qualifying dignity-notable hits (new)
3. GPT prose — Year Ahead Reading (unchanged)
4. SR Advance Preview — "When This Year Intensifies" (unchanged)

## Specifications

### Eligibility rules (what qualifies for the section)

1. **Angular houses:** Any SR planet (from the ten classical planets in `PLANET_NAMES`: Sun, Moon, Mercury, Venus, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune, Pluto) in SR house 1, 4, 7, or 10 qualifies automatically.

2. **Luminary exclusion:** SR Sun and SR Moon are already shown by `SRStaticBriefs` and must not be duplicated in the new section, even if they fall in an angular house.

3. **Minimum meaningful planets only:** Only the ten classical planets (`PLANET_NAMES`) are eligible. Asteroids (Chiron, Ceres, Pallas, Juno, Vesta) are excluded from this section despite having `PLANET_IN_HOUSE` entries — angular asteroid placements in a solar return carry less annual weight and would dilute the curated feel.

4. **`PLANET_IN_HOUSE` key existence check:** A placement only generates a card if `PLANET_IN_HOUSE[`${planet.name}_H${planet.house}`]?.brief` resolves to a non-empty string. If a key is absent (should not occur for the ten classical planets across houses 1–12 given the current data, but must be defensive), that placement is silently skipped.

5. **NorthNode exclusion:** NorthNode is filtered from `srChart.planets` by the existing `SRPlanetTable` (line 299 pattern: `filter(p => p.name !== 'NorthNode')`). Apply the same filter here.

6. **Maximum card count:** Cap the section at **6 cards** to prevent visual flooding if many planets cluster in angular houses. When more than 6 qualify, apply the following priority sort and take the top 6:
   - Priority 1: slow planets in angular houses (Saturn, Jupiter, Uranus, Neptune, Pluto) — these carry the most annual weight.
   - Priority 2: fast planets in angular houses (Mercury, Venus, Mars).
   - Within each tier, preserve the order: Saturn, Jupiter, Uranus, Neptune, Pluto for slow; Mercury, Venus, Mars for fast.

7. **Empty section:** If zero eligible placements remain after applying rules 1–6, the entire section is not rendered (return `null`). Do not show a section header with empty content.

### Component structure

8. Implement as a new component named `SRThemeBriefs` in `SolarReturnPage.tsx`, following the same file-local pattern as `SRStaticBriefs`, `KeyPlacements`, and `SolarReturnAdvancePreview`. No new files.

9. `SRThemeBriefs` accepts a single prop: `srData: SolarReturnData`.

10. Derive eligible placements using a `useMemo` that filters and sorts `srData.srChart.planets` per the eligibility rules above. The memo depends only on `srData.srChart`.

11. Place `<SRThemeBriefs srData={solarReturnData} />` immediately after `<SRStaticBriefs srData={solarReturnData} />` in the Reading tab (line ~518 of the current file). No other layout changes.

### Card visual design

12. The section opens with a heading:
    ```
    <h3 class="font-heading text-amber-300/80 text-xs uppercase tracking-wider mb-3">
      This Year's Themes
    </h3>
    ```
    The heading style must be consistent with existing section headers elsewhere on the page (e.g., the `OverviewStrip` header uses `text-amber-300 text-sm uppercase tracking-wider`; use the slightly subdued `text-amber-300/80 text-xs` to keep it subordinate to the main GPT prose heading).

13. Cards lay out in a **single-column list** (not a grid). Each card is a `div` with:
    - Background: `bg-amber-900/5` (same as the existing `SRStaticBriefs` cards)
    - Border: `border border-amber-500/10 rounded-lg px-4 py-3 mb-2`
    - Left-to-right horizontal layout within each card

14. Inside each card, left to right:
    - **Planet glyph** (`PLANET_GLYPHS[planet.name]`): `text-amber-400 text-base mr-2 shrink-0` (matches glyph usage in `SRPlanetTable`)
    - **Planet name + house label** (`SR {name} in House {house}`): `text-amber-300/80 font-heading text-sm font-semibold` — this is the card title
    - **Brief text** (from `PLANET_IN_HOUSE[key].brief`): `text-mystic-text/70 text-sm leading-relaxed mt-0.5` prefixed with "This year: " (same prefix used in `SRStaticBriefs`)

15. Do not show the `detail` field from `InterpretationEntry` — only `brief`. The section must remain scannable; the GPT prose provides depth.

16. No expand/collapse toggle on individual cards. These are static briefs; interactivity is not needed here.

17. Do not show retrograde status on individual cards. Retrograde context is already visible in the planet table.

### Framing and prose

18. The section heading must read **"This Year's Themes"** exactly (not "Angular Placements," not "SR Planets," not "Themes of the Year").

19. Each card's brief is prefixed with "This year: " (note the colon and space), identical to the prefix used in the existing `SRStaticBriefs` cards (line 382 of current file: `This year: {sunBrief}`). This prefix is essential: the `PLANET_IN_HOUSE` briefs are written for natal charts in second-person ("you are…", "your…") and the prefix re-anchors them to the annual Solar Return frame.

20. No additional prose or explanatory copy above or below the cards. The section header and the cards are self-explanatory.

### Data and performance

21. All computation is synchronous — `PLANET_IN_HOUSE` is a static in-memory record, and `srData` is already in state when the Reading tab renders. No async calls, no loading state, no skeleton UI for this section.

22. The `useMemo` must be stable: it must not recompute unless `srData.srChart` changes. Because `srData` itself changes on year selection, this is automatically correct as long as the dependency is `srData.srChart` (or `srData` if that is simpler to maintain).

23. The section must render and be visible before the GPT prose resolves. It is pure static data and must not be gated on `solarReturnInterpretation` state.

### Accessibility

24. The section wrapper must carry `aria-label="This year's SR themes"` for screen reader navigation.

25. Each card's planet name and house must be readable as text (not glyph-only). The glyph is decorative; the planet name text provides the accessible label. Do not use `aria-hidden` on the planet name text.

26. Minimum tap target for any interactive element within cards: 44 × 44 px. Since these cards have no interactive elements (they are purely informational), no special sizing is required, but maintain comfortable `py-3` padding.

### Edge cases

27. **SR chart with all planets in non-angular houses:** The section renders nothing (`return null`). The space between `SRStaticBriefs` and the GPT prose collapses naturally.

28. **SR chart with Sun or Moon in an angular house (e.g., Moon in H10):** Moon is already shown by `SRStaticBriefs` and must not appear in `SRThemeBriefs`. The filter must exclude `Sun` and `Moon` by name, not by house.

29. **Unknown birth time (srChart.unknownTime === true):** When birth time is unknown, SR house placements are unreliable (houses are calculated using a default noon time). Do not suppress the section for unknown-time charts, but add a small footnote below the section:
    ```
    <p class="text-mystic-muted/60 text-xs mt-2 italic">
      House placements are approximate — birth time was not provided.
    </p>
    ```
    This matches the existing unknown-time disclosure pattern used elsewhere in the product (see `OverviewStrip` prop `unknownTime` at line 156).

30. **Year change (e.g., user switches from current year to next year):** `solarReturnData` is replaced wholesale by the dispatch chain. The component naturally re-renders with new data; no additional handling is needed.

31. **PLANET_IN_HOUSE key missing for a qualifying planet:** Skip silently. Do not render a card with undefined text. The `?.brief` optional chaining provides this safety.

32. **All ten non-luminary planets in angular houses (extreme case, practically impossible but theoretically possible):** The 6-card cap applies; the priority sort in spec 6 determines which 6 are shown.

### Acceptance checks

33. Given an SR chart with Saturn in H10 and Jupiter in H1 (and no other angular planets besides Sun/Moon): the section renders exactly two cards — "SR Saturn in House 10" and "SR Jupiter in House 1" — with their respective briefs. Sun and Moon are absent from this section even if they are in H1 or H10.

34. Given an SR chart with all planets in non-angular houses (cadent or succedent only, excluding Sun and Moon): `SRThemeBriefs` renders nothing and no section heading is visible.

35. Given `srChart.unknownTime === true` and at least one qualifying planet in an angular house: the section renders with cards plus the "House placements are approximate" footnote.

36. The section is visible and fully rendered before the GPT interpretation resolves (i.e., it is not conditionally rendered inside the `solarReturnInterpretation !== null` block).

37. On a narrow viewport (375 px width), all cards are legible and no text overflows horizontally. The single-column layout naturally accommodates narrow screens.

38. The section does not appear on the Chart tab — it is scoped exclusively to the Reading tab content block.

## Out of Scope

- Asteroids (Chiron, Ceres, Pallas, Juno, Vesta) in SR angular houses — excluded from this pass despite having `PLANET_IN_HOUSE` entries. Can be revisited in a future sprint.
- Succedent houses (2, 5, 8, 11) — not shown here even though they are meaningfully important in SR interpretation. The priority for this section is angular concentration, not exhaustiveness.
- SR Ascendant and MC sign briefs — the existing `KeyPlacements` component already names the SR ASC and MC signs. Adding sign-level briefs for the angles (separate from planet-in-house briefs) is a distinct feature and out of scope.
- Dignity analysis (exaltation, detriment, fall) — the stub mentioned "dignity-notable placements" as a secondary axis. After reviewing the data layer, the `PLANET_IN_HOUSE` keys do not encode dignity; computing dignity would require new engine logic. This is explicitly deferred.
- SR-specific rewriting of the `PLANET_IN_HOUSE` briefs — the briefs are written for natal context ("your Sun in the 10th house…"). The "This year: " prefix provides sufficient reframing for an SR context without requiring a new data file. Creating SR-specific brief copy is out of scope.
- The SR advance preview position — the vision.md note about evaluating whether `SolarReturnAdvancePreview` should move higher on the Reading tab is a separate layout decision. This proposal does not relocate the advance preview strip.
- Any changes to the Chart tab or the planet table layout.
- GPT prompt changes for the SR interpretation.

## Open Questions

1. **Should the section title include the year?** E.g., "Themes for {targetYear}" instead of "This Year's Themes." The year is already prominent in the page header, so the generic label may be sufficient. Preference is for the generic label; revisit only if user testing shows confusion when two SR years are compared.

2. **Should cadent houses be included for slow planets?** A Saturn or Pluto in house 9 or 3 carries more annual meaning than Mercury in house 1. The current spec uses angular-only for simplicity and curatorial tightness. If the product feel is that the section is too sparse for most users' charts, expanding to slow planets in all houses (but still capped at 6) is the natural extension.

3. **Unknown-time footnote placement:** The spec places the footnote below all cards. An alternative is a single banner above the cards. The existing `OverviewStrip` renders its unknown-time note inline. Implementer may choose whichever is cleaner given the surrounding layout.

4. **Long briefs:** Some `PLANET_IN_HOUSE` briefs (particularly Chiron, Ceres, Juno, Vesta) are very long — but since those asteroids are excluded here, all qualifying briefs (Sun–Pluto) are single short sentences (e.g., "Ambitious career drive" for Mars_H10). No truncation logic is needed, but the implementer should visually verify that no brief wraps unexpectedly on narrow viewports.
