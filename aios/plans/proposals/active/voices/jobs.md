# Steve Jobs — Proposal Voice

I've been using this app. It's genuinely beautiful — the chart wheel glows, the gold accents feel right, the typography has gravitas. But I'm sitting here thinking: this feels like a collection of features, not a *suite*. When I open it, I feel like I'm navigating a filing cabinet. What I want is to feel like I'm entering a *world*.

The dream interpretation is buried in a modal. Numerology doesn't exist. The daily snapshot is a card. None of these things *talk to each other*. They coexist, but they don't *sing together*.

Here's what I think:

**The most urgent thing is numerology.** Not because numerology is the biggest feature — it's not. It's because numerology is the *bridge*. When someone gets their Life Path number and realizes it rhymes with their Moon sign, something clicks. "Oh. This is real. These systems are saying the same thing in different languages." That cross-resonance is the emotional core of an integrated suite. Without it, you have astrology. With it, you have a *cosmological identity portrait*.

The numerology integration shouldn't be a sidebar or a tab. It should be woven through the reading. "Your Life Path 7 and your Mercury in Scorpio 12th house both speak to a mind that seeks hidden truths. The solitude you crave isn't weakness — it's your mode of knowing." That's not a feature. That's a revelation.

**The second thing I keep coming back to is the solar return chart.** Every person has a birthday. And every astrologer knows that the birthday chart — the solar return — is one of the most practical tools in the practice. It answers: "What is this year of my life actually about?" Not transits, not generalities — the *specific year* ahead. This is enormously emotionally valuable. It's also a clear, bounded deliverable that doesn't require a ton of new infrastructure.

**The dream journal is a missed opportunity.** Right now dreams disappear after a session. That's wrong. Dreams are cumulative — the themes that repeat across weeks and months are the significant ones. A dream journal that persists, and eventually synthesizes: "You've had water dreams 4 times this month. Neptune is transiting your 12th house." That's the kind of thing that makes someone cry because it's so true.

**Cut one thing that's cluttering the experience:** the SkyTodayChart on the landing page renders today's sky at Greenwich (0°, 0°). It's decorative but it's *lying* — it's not actually *your* sky. It should either be removed or replaced with something personalized. Decorative lies dilute trust.

What this product needs is not more features. It needs the features it has to *resonate together*. Numerology + astrology cross-interpretation is the key that unlocks the suite feeling.
