# Steve Jobs — Voice Analysis: Sprint 0011

Sprint 0010 did what it promised. The transit rows speak now. The timeline is house-aware. The daily pill sentence lands before the GPT paragraph loads. The plumbing is complete. I have looked at what is left, and I want to tell you what I see.

What I see is a set of reading surfaces that are all missing the same last inch. The data arrives. The computation runs. And then — right at the edge of where the user is standing — the product goes quiet. Not because the interpretation doesn't exist, but because no one walked it across the gap.

There are five places where this is most visible. Let me name them precisely.

---

## The Fundamental Problem: Relational Surfaces Have No Voice

Sprint 0010 gave transit readings a voice. The shared `AspectRow` component exists. The briefs compute. The pill sentence works. All of that is in place.

But the product has three surfaces that are the most emotionally loaded readings it offers — synastry aspects, the house overlay section, couple transits — and every single one of them is silent. A person who enters their birth data and their partner's birth data is doing something that matters to them. They are not querying a database. They are asking: what does the sky say about this relationship?

And the product hands them a table.

The `SynastryAspectsSection` in `SynastryPage.tsx` renders every cross-chart aspect as a bare row: glyphs, symbol, names, orb, nature badge. No sentence. No brief. No expand toggle. After sprint 0010, every *transit* aspect row has an expand/collapse with a house-aware brief. The pattern is built. The component exists at `src/components/reading/AspectRow.tsx`. It is not being used on this page.

The `HouseOverlaySection` in the same file is a table that renders "Your Venus falls in their 7th House" — and nothing else. `src/data/interpretations/houseThemes.ts` has the theme for House 7: *Marriage, partnerships, open enemies*. The planet Venus has an archetype. The sentence "your Venus falls in their house of partnership — you carry beauty and affection directly into the space where they seek a committed bond" is computable from data that already exists. The section renders no such sentence.

The `TransitAspectsToComposite` section in `SynastryTransitPage.tsx` is the pre-sprint-0010 state of the transit rows. No expand/collapse. No brief. A person checking how the current sky affects their relationship as a unit gets rows with glyphs and orbs and no interpretation.

These three gaps are the same gap. The pattern to fix them exists. It is a matter of applying it where it is currently absent.

## Proposal 1: Synastry Aspect Rows — Write the Relational Database

This is the highest-leverage work in the sprint. Not because it is the most technically interesting, but because it is the surface where interpretation matters most.

The existing `ASPECT_INTERPRETATIONS` table in `src/data/interpretations/aspectInterpretations.ts` is written in natal voice. "Your will and emotional nature." "Your capacity for joy." These phrasings are correct for a natal reading and wrong for a synastry row. A synastry brief needs relational voice: *your Venus trine their Moon — you bring warmth, they receive it without walls.* That sentence cannot be generated by rephrasing a natal entry.

The sprint vision is correct that the right path is a thin new data table: `synastryAspectBriefs.ts`. It does not need to be large. The high-frequency pairs that appear in almost every synastry reading are: Sun-Moon, Venus-Mars, Moon-Moon, Mercury-Mercury, Sun-Venus, Moon-Venus, Mars-Mars, Saturn-Moon, Saturn-Sun, Jupiter-Moon, Jupiter-Venus, Neptune-Moon, Pluto-Moon, Venus-Venus. That is roughly 14 core pairs. Across 5 aspect types (conjunction, sextile, square, trine, opposition) and accounting for person1/person2 directionality where it matters, a 30–40 entry table covers the cases that appear in nearly every reading.

For everything outside that set — an unusual cross-chart contact like Pluto opposite Jupiter — the fallback should be exactly the pattern the vision describes: aspect-type archetype plus planet-pair archetype, assembled into a relational sentence.

The `AspectRow` component at `src/components/reading/AspectRow.tsx` handles the expand/collapse, the nature color, the brief reveal, the border styling — all of it. The only missing piece is: `SynastryAspectsSection` does not call it. The fix is to compute a relational brief for each row and pass it in as the `brief` prop.

This is not a large surface to cover. It is a precise, focused piece of writing work followed by a straightforward component wiring. The writing is what matters. Two sentences per high-frequency pair, written in relational voice, that say something specific about what this cross-chart contact means for two people. Not "Venus trine Moon is harmonious." That is the label. The brief is: "your affection finds natural welcome — the warmth you extend is received without question or defense."

That passes the quality bar. That is the product speaking.

## Proposal 2: House Overlay Section — One Sentence Per Entry

The house overlay section in `SynastryPage.tsx` is the most emotionally resonant section on the synastry page and the most silent. "Person 1's Planets in Person 2's Houses" is a table with three columns: planet, sign, house number. The user reads "Venus — Libra — House 7" and has to know what that means themselves.

The fix is narrow and achievable: for each row in `HouseOverlaySection`, compose one sentence using the planet's archetype and the house's theme from `HOUSE_THEMES`. The `getHouseTheme` function at `src/data/interpretations/houseThemes.ts` gives the house name and theme in one call. The planet archetypes are already embedded in the existing interpretation data — Venus is beauty and affection and love-seeking; Saturn is structure and limitation and commitment; Mars is drive and assertion. A lookup table of 10 planet archetypes takes twenty lines to write.

The composed sentence: "Your [planet archetype] falls in their [house name] — [one clause about what that means relationally]." For Venus in their 7th: "Your capacity for beauty and affection falls directly in their house of committed partnership — you represent what they seek in a long-term bond." For Saturn in their 4th: "Your disciplining energy falls in their house of home and emotional foundation — they may feel both steadied and limited by the structures you bring."

The house overlay section currently renders the data and communicates nothing. Adding one sentence per row does not change the layout, does not add a GPT call, does not require a new engine calculation. It requires writing 12 × 10 = 120 relational planet-in-house phrases and wiring them into the render. Most of these do not need to be unique — Venus in houses 1 through 12 for a partner's chart follows a predictable relational arc. The writing is mechanical but the impact on the user is not.

## Proposal 3: Couple Transit Rows — Apply the Pattern That Already Exists

The `TransitAspectsToComposite` section in `SynastryTransitPage.tsx` is literally the pre-sprint-0010 version of the transit rows. Static. No expand toggle. No brief. Glyphs, orb, nature badge, done.

This is the simplest fix in the sprint. The `AspectRow` component already handles everything. The composite natal planet carries a house number from the composite chart. The brief for a composite transit should address the relationship as a unit: "Saturn pressing on your composite 3rd house asks you to examine how you communicate as a couple — where words have been avoided, avoided, or turned into walls."

That phrasing is achievable with the same `computeTransitAspectBrief` function used in the transit reading, adapted for composite-voice output. The composite chart's planet house tells you which area of the relationship is activated. The transit planet's nature tells you the character of the activation. The sentence writes itself from those two facts.

The change is: replace the static div rows in `TransitAspectsToComposite` with `AspectRow` components, pass a computed brief for each row. The brief computation follows the same house-aware pattern used in `src/data/interpretations/transitAspectBriefs.ts`, with composite-voice phrasing ("your relationship's" rather than "your").

## Proposal 4: Solar Return — Give the User Something Before GPT Loads

The Solar Return page has a structural problem I want to name directly. The `Reading` tab has two states: loading skeleton and GPT text. Nothing in between. A user who opens the reading tab before GPT responds sees a skeleton that says "Tracking the Sun's return..." and nothing else.

The SR Sun house and SR Moon house are the two most important facts on the page. They are displayed in the `KeyPlacements` grid above the tab toggle: SR Sun in House 3, SR Moon in House 7. But these placements are shown as data labels — "Sun: Gemini H3 — Primary focus" — without any sentence explaining what SR Sun in House 3 means for the year.

`src/data/interpretations/planetInHouse.ts` already has `Sun_H3` and all other `Sun_H*` and `Moon_H*` entries. The fix is to surface the `detail` text from `Sun_H${srSun.house}` and `Moon_H${srMoon.house}` as static briefs on the Reading tab, rendered above the GPT skeleton. The user sees something specific and accurate immediately — before the network call returns.

This is one-paragraph-per-placement, static, client-side, zero-latency. It does not replace the GPT reading. It gives the user a foothold while they wait, and for users who hit their GPT limit, it gives them the two most important facts from the year-ahead chart in plain language.

Additionally — and the sprint vision is precise about this — `buildSolarReturnPrompt` in `src/engine/solarReturn.ts` does not include the dominant element profile. `buildTransitPrompt` does, post-sprint-0010. The SR prompt is sending complete planetary data but not giving GPT the synthesized element analysis that helps it frame the year's character. `analyzeElements` already exists in the codebase. Adding its output to the SR prompt is two lines in `buildSolarReturnPrompt`.

## Proposal 5: Synastry GPT Prompt — Complete the Symmetry

Sprint 0010 upgraded `buildTransitPrompt`: element profiles, priority instruction to lead with the tightest aspect, anti-cliché constraints, house-number life-area naming. The synastry prompt in `src/engine/synastry.ts` received partial upgrades (aspects sorted by orb) but not the full treatment.

The `buildSynastryPrompt` function does not prepend element profiles for either person. It does not instruct GPT to name the life area alongside house numbers. It does not tell GPT which pair to lead with. The transit prompt was repaired; the synastry prompt is still structurally incomplete by comparison.

The fix is the same two additions that went into `buildTransitPrompt`. First: call `analyzeElements` for both charts and prepend the element profile blocks before the aspect list. Second: add a priority instruction: "Lead with the tightest Venus-Mars or Sun-Moon contact in the synastry, naming both planets and the aspect type. Do not open with general compatibility framing. Begin with the specific contact that defines the emotional core of this relationship." Third: for each aspect, add the house context for both planets where birth times are known: "Person 1's Venus in their 5th house contacts Person 2's Moon in their 1st house — name what this means for what Person 1 brings to Person 2's self-expression."

These are prompt-text additions to `src/engine/synastry.ts`. No type changes. No logic changes. No new GPT calls. The symmetry with the transit prompt is the argument: we applied these improvements to the transit reading because they worked. Apply them here for the same reason.

## What Is Shallow and Must Be Refused

I want to be explicit about what sprint 0011 must not do.

Do not add a new page. Do not add a new reading type. Do not add a new GPT endpoint. The reading surfaces exist. Every one of them has gaps that need to be closed. Once closed, the product will be something people want to return to. Before they are closed, adding new doors to rooms that are already empty is the wrong move.

Do not redesign the visual layout. The dark-mystic palette is correct. The Cormorant Garamond headings are correct. The gold accents against dark backgrounds are the right choice. The gap is not in how things look. It is in what they say.

Do not modify the natal interpretation databases that are not intended for relational use. `planetInHouse.ts`, `planetInSign.ts`, `aspectInterpretations.ts` are written in natal voice. Using them unchanged for synastry briefs is audible as wrong to any user who reads carefully. The new synastry relational brief table should be written fresh, in relational voice, even if it covers a narrower set of pairs. Breadth of coverage is less important than correctness of voice.

## The Quality Bar for Sprint 0011

A user opens the synastry reading for themselves and someone they love. They scroll to the aspects section. They tap a row. Two sentences appear that describe, in relational language, what that cross-chart contact means for *these two people*. Not planet archetypes. Not generic compatibility language. Something specific: what does it feel like when your Venus contacts their Saturn? What does it mean that your Mars falls exactly on their natal Sun? The expanded brief answers that question.

A user looks at the house overlay section. They see "Your Moon falls in their 4th House." Beneath it, a single sentence: something that tells them what it means to carry their emotional nature directly into their partner's house of home and roots. They do not need to consult an astrology textbook to understand what they're reading.

A user opens the Solar Return reading for the upcoming year. Before the GPT paragraph loads, they see two paragraphs: one for the SR Sun house and one for the SR Moon house. They know, immediately, what the primary focus of the year is and what the emotional climate will be. The GPT paragraph adds synthesis and nuance. It does not have to explain what the SR Sun house is.

That is the standard. These surfaces know something about the user. They should say it.

The product computes more than it communicates. Sprint 0011 closes five of the largest remaining gaps. After this sprint, someone who only reads the static layer — no GPT, no expand toggles — should still walk away understanding something specific about their chart, their partner's chart, and what the year ahead holds. That is the product we should be building.
