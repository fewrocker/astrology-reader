# Hayao Miyazaki — Proposal Voice

There is something sacred being attempted here. I feel it in the gold accents and the dark backgrounds. Someone cared about this.

But there are places where the care runs out, and that's where I want to focus.

**The SkyTodayChart on the landing page.** It shows the sky at Greenwich coordinates — a placeholder, not a person's sky. The user looks at it and perhaps feels the mystical quality of seeing planets arranged in a wheel. But when they learn it is not their sky, not even today's sky at their location, something small but real is broken. A product that claims to read your soul should not open with a generic placeholder. Either personalize it — show them *their current transits*, their natal chart with today's sky overlaid — or replace it with something honest. A beautiful but impersonal image is better than a false personal one.

**The dream feature asks for so little and receives so much.** When a user types their dream and receives an interpretation woven from their natal chart, current transits, and the symbolism of the dream itself — that is a genuinely magical moment. But the magic disappears when the session ends. Dreams have memory. The sleeping mind returns to the same waters again and again. A dream journal — even a simple one — would honor this. Let users see their dreams from last week and last month. Let the product notice "you've dreamed of running away three times." That noticing is care.

**Numerology belongs here.** I do not say this only because users might want it. I say it because numerology and astrology are *sisters* — two ancient systems that map the same invisible terrain. When a user's Life Path number resonates with their chart, when their personal year number echoes the transits they're living through, the experience deepens. The product stops being "a chart reader" and becomes something more rare: a system of mirrors that all point the same direction. That convergence is beautiful and true, and the user feels it.

**The solar return is the birthday reading.** What gift could be more personal? On someone's birthday — or before it — they might want to know: what is this new year of my life about? The solar return chart answers that with specificity and care. It requires no new form inputs, no new data. Just the natal chart and the current year. The implementation cost is low. The emotional value is high.

**Small detail I noticed:** the prompt sent to GPT for daily snapshots ends with "Be direct and personal." The best interpreters are not direct in a harsh way — they are direct in a *seeing* way. A small refinement to the system prompt could raise the quality of every AI interpretation. This costs nothing but thought.

What this product needs most is not new features. It needs the existing features to feel like they belong together — like rooms in the same home rather than stalls at a market. Numerology is the connective tissue that makes everything else feel like one system.
